Deadpool Review

Andrew Lowson Â· February 11, 2016

Deadpool is a movie 11 years in the making and I think it benefits from the wait. This is the first of seven comic book movies coming out this year, including two versus movies and an end of the world disaster film. This ultra-violent superhero comedy is just what we need as a palette cleanser before those arrive this summer.

This is Ryan Reynolds fifth outing in a comic book movie after appearing in Blade: Trinity, X-Men Origins: Wolverine, Green Lantern and R.I.P.D, each movie just as awful as the next. He has a track record for starring in terrible comic book films and he has finally broken that.

Marketing

It’s worth pointing out the incredible marketing effort for this movie. From the SkullPoopL and Rom-Com parody billboards to Deadpool popping up for Australia Day to poke fun at Hugh Jackman/Wolverine, the marketing has knocked it out of the park.

The Good

I love this movie. It is fast paced, it never lulls, it is continuously funny and gives Deadpool fans everything they expect from the character, and will surprise the ones expecting your typical comic book movie.

The humour really sets this film apart. It almost comes across as parody because it’s so self-deprecating of the genre but it definitely falls more in line with Scream than Scary Movie. Deadpool knows he’s in a comic book movie and knows he’s played by Ryan Reynolds and the script takes full advantage of this. This allows them to break the fourth wall, make references to the studio, other superhero movies, the low budget and more. Nobody is safe, not even Reynolds himself.

Whilst the plot is a very typical origin story, the scenes aren’t presented chronologically so it doesn’t suffer from superhero origin-syndrome where the first 45 minutes are just foreplay to the full costume reveal. The film begins with the scene from the leaked test footage, dropping you into a fully formed Deadpool and then stops to draw breath and takes you through the story, filling in details as required. It’s a brilliant decision that stops large periods from being really dark, which would imbalance the overall tone of the movie. It’s a revenge story that doesn’t pull the punches, it delivers as promised a very violent and incredibly witty movie.

Deadpool joins a short list of superhero movies that don’t get stuck in a sandbox of the genre. The Dark Knight is crime thriller taking influence from Heat, Captain America: The Winter Soldier is a spy-thriller and Deadpool is a revenge movie.

The Less Good

Every superhero has to come up with their name and the dialogue where this happens was almost as cringe-worthy as in Fant4stic. I let it pass because the rest of the scene was funny and entertaining, and on the whole the script was incredible so in 100 minutes I’ll allow one misstep considering I can hardly call myself a writer.

I think if you’re not a fan of crass humour, dick jokes or superhero movies, the humour in the film will really rub you the wrong way, but the marketing probably did a good job at steering that group of people away.

Overall

As I said before, I love this movie and I will probably see it again soon. I think it’s great that it has finally come to cinemas after the long wait and it certainly appears that everyone involved is very proud of the result.

Deadpool doesn’t take itself too seriously, it is not another end of the world plot, it has small scale with a focused and fairly simple plot that just aims to please the audience during throughout it’s ~100 minute runtime. It’s my favourite movie of 2016 so far…ok it’s the only movie I’ve seen this year, but it’s still amazing.
